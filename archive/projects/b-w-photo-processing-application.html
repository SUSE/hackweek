    • B&W photo processing application

    Tags: Desktop, Graphics, F-Spot, Photography, InProgress

    Description

    Making a digital black and white photograph takes much more than just doing "Desaturate" in GIMP. It involves selecting channels, manipulating the overall contrast, selectively brightening and darkening regions of the photo, and applying a tint. It's more or less what's done in a chemical darkroom, only with more fine control, and it doesn't smell.

    I have developed a [[http://schani.wordpress.com/2006/05/23/my-digital-bw-workflow/|Photoshop workflow]] for my B&W processing, but I'd rather have something Free that runs on Linux. Unfortunately, The GIMP isn't up to the task because it's missing adjustment layers.

    What I plan to do is implement something very analogous to my workflow, using [[http://www.gegl.org/|GEGL]] as the imaging core and C#/Gtk#/Gnome# for the UI. Ultimately it should probably be integrated with F-Spot.
